The Process

How Affiliate Links Work

  1. Step 1: A Link Is Clearly Presented

    Some buttons or text links may direct visitors to a third-party provider.

  2. Step 2: The Visitor Chooses Whether to Continue

    Using an affiliate link is optional. Visitors may review the provider directly or choose a different service.

  3. Step 3: A Qualifying Action May Take Place

    If the visitor purchases or signs up after following an eligible link, the provider may record the referral.

  4. Step 4: The Website May Receive Commission

    The provider may pay Hosting Stack UK a commission without normally adding an extra charge to the visitor’s purchase.

Tracking periods, eligibility requirements and commission arrangements vary between affiliate programmes.
Our Editorial Approach

How We Make Recommendations

The recommendations published on this website are based on independent research, practical evaluation and careful consideration of the features, performance and overall value of the tools and services discussed.

Our aim is to assess solutions using factors that matter to online businesses, website owners and developers, including speed, security, reliability, scalability, support quality and long-term usability.

Every website and business has different needs. For that reason, our content is designed to provide balanced insights rather than one-size-fits-all recommendations.

Where possible, we aim to highlight both the advantages and limitations of the products and services we discuss. This allows visitors to make decisions based on their own goals, technical requirements and budgets.

Affiliate relationships do not remove our commitment to clear disclosure, careful research and balanced presentation.

Evaluation Factors

What We Consider

Performance
Whether the service is designed to support fast and consistent website performance.
Reliability
How the service approaches availability, infrastructure and ongoing operation.
Security
The protective features, backups, monitoring and recovery options included.
Scalability
Whether the service can support increasing traffic, websites or technical demands.
Support
The availability, relevance and expected quality of customer or technical assistance.
Ease of Management
How simple the platform is to configure, maintain and use.
Pricing and Value
How the cost compares with the features, support and intended audience.
Suitability
Which types of users, websites or businesses are most likely to receive meaningful value.
A product may be strong in one area while being unsuitable in another. Our aim is to explain those differences clearly.
Editorial Transparency

Our Commitment to Transparency

Hosting Stack UK aims to make affiliate relationships visible and understandable.

Where affiliate links are used, we aim to provide an appropriate disclosure close to the relevant content as well as this full disclosure page.

We also aim to distinguish between factual information, general guidance and our own evaluation of a product’s suitability.

Affiliate commissions may support the website, but visitors should never feel pressured to purchase a service that does not meet their needs.

If a lower-cost option, different type of hosting or alternative solution may be more suitable, our content should make that clear.

Our Transparency Commitments

  • Display affiliate disclosures clearly
  • Discuss relevant limitations
  • Avoid fabricated urgency
  • Avoid guaranteed results
  • Encourage visitors to confirm current details
  • Recommend according to likely suitability
  • Correct material inaccuracies when identified
Before You Purchase

Important Information for Visitors

Hosting plans, prices, promotional offers, product features and affiliate arrangements can change over time.

Before purchasing or registering for a service, visitors should confirm the latest information directly with the provider.

Hosting Stack UK provides general educational information and does not provide personalised legal, financial or technical advice.

A product or service discussed on this website may not be suitable for every visitor.

Your decision should consider:

  • Website type
  • Expected traffic
  • Budget
  • Technical requirements
  • Support needs
  • Growth plans
  • Contract terms
  • Renewal pricing
  • Cancellation conditions
  • Data and privacy requirements

Taking time to compare suitability, current pricing and contract terms can help prevent costly mismatches.
